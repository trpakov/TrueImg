An invisible image watermark based on the discrete cosine transform (DCT) is a form of digital watermarking that is used to embed a unique signature into an image. The DCT is a mathematical transformation that is used to convert an image from the spatial domain to the frequency domain, where it can be analyzed and manipulated more easily.

Invisible image watermarks based on DCT are widely used for authentication and protection of digital images, as they are difficult to remove and can provide a high level of security. They are also compatible with a wide range of image file formats and can be easily implemented using software tools.
